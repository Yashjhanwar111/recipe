Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • Mawa (Khoya) ‚Äì 1 cup (grated)
  • Maida (All-purpose flour) ‚Äì 2 tbsp
  • Milk powder ‚Äì 2 tbsp (optional)
  • Baking soda ‚Äì 1/4 tsp
  • Milk ‚Äì As required (for kneading)
  • Ghee/Oil ‚Äì For deep frying

Process

  1. Make Sugar Syrup: Boil 1.5 cups sugar with 1 cup water, add cardamom, saffron (optional), rose water (optional), and lemon juice. Simmer until slightly sticky.
  2. Prepare Dough: Mash 1 cup khoya, mix with 2 tbsp maida, 2 tbsp milk powder (optional), and 1/4 tsp baking soda
  3. Knead & Rest: Add milk gradually, knead into a soft dough, and let it rest for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Shape Balls: Make small, smooth balls without cracks.
  5. Fry: Heat ghee/oil on low-medium flame and fry till golden brown.
  6. Soak in Syrup: Add hot Gulab Jamuns to warm sugar syrup and let them soak for 2-3 hours.
  7. Serve: Garnish with pistachios/almonds and enjoy warm or chilled! üòç

Shrikhand

Ingredients

  • Hung curd (Chakka dahi) ‚Äì 2 cups (strained yogurt)
  • Powdered sugar ‚Äì 1/2 cup (adjust to taste)
  • Cardamom powder ‚Äì 1/2 tsp
  • Saffron strands ‚Äì Few strands (soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)

Process

  1. Prepare Hung Curd: Strain 2 cups yogurt in a muslin cloth for 4-6 hours to remove excess water.
  2. Soak Saffron: Mix saffron strands in 2 tbsp warm milk and set aside.
  3. Whisk Curd: Transfer hung curd to a bowl and whisk until smooth and creamy
  4. Add Sugar & Flavor: Mix in 1/2 cup powdered sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron milk. Whisk well.
  5. Chill: Refrigerate for 1-2 hours for better flavor.
  6. Garnish & Serve: Top with chopped nuts, dry fruits, and optionally add rose water.

Milk Cake

Ingredients

  • Full-fat milk ‚Äì 1 liter
  • Lemon juice/Vinegar ‚Äì 1 tbsp (for curdling milk)
  • Sugar ‚Äì 1/2 cup (adjust to taste)
  • Ghee ‚Äì 2 tbsp

Process

  1. Boil Milk: Heat 1 liter full-fat milk in a heavy-bottom pan and bring it to a boil.
  2. Curdle Milk: Add 1 tbsp lemon juice/vinegar and stir until milk curdles.
  3. Drain Water: Let it rest for 5 minutes, then strain the chhena (curdled milk) using a muslin cloth. Do not wash it.
  4. Cook the Mixture: Transfer back to the pan, add 1/2 cup sugar and 2 tbsp ghee. Stir continuously on low flame.
  5. Achieve Grainy Texture: Cook until it thickens, turns slightly brown, and leaves the sides of the pan.
  6. Set & Rest: Transfer to a greased plate or mold, press gently, and let it cool for 5-6 hours for proper texture.
  7. Garnish & Serve: Cut into pieces, garnish with chopped nuts, and enjoy! üòç
